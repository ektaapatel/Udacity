Introduction¶

No-Shows of appointments are a bitter reality in any medical practice. Not only it impacts the daily work flow of an operations but it can also cause financial issues in any type of organization. One cannot prevent patients from No-Showing for their appointments but the occurrences of No-Shows can be predicted or even reduced if you have the right data in hand.
In this project, I will work on a dataset that collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil. It is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. I will base my analysis on the dataset and provide the insight on why and when an indivudual No-Shows or Shows up for an appointment.

Posing Quetions:¶

  1. Does age have any relations with no-showing for an appointment?
    • Do seniors tend to no-show more?
  2. Do the patients who are NOT enrolled in Brasilian welfare program tend to no-show more than who are enrolled and vice versa?
  3. What proportion of patients No-Shows who receives reminder calls vs who do not receive one?
  4. Does patients medical condition/s have anything to do with No-Showing for their appointments?
  5. Does the gap between the Schedule Date vs Appointment Date cause No-Shows?
  6. what are the proportion of patients No-Showing weekday vs weekends?

def crosstab(dataset, group_by):
    ct = pd.crosstab(index = dataset[group_by], columns = dataset.NoShow).reset_index()
    ct['ns_prob'] = (ct[1] / (ct[0] + ct[1]))*100
    return ct[[group_by, 'ns_prob']]

Conclusion:¶

In this project, the data analysis process was conducted to predict the likelihood of a person no showing for their appointments. As mentioned previously, it is impossible to prevent a person from no showing for their appointments. However, with given data and imposing the right questions, the likelihood of no-shows can be predictable. Additionally, the true offenders (human or technology) can be identified to form a no-show policy i.e. charging a certain amount for no shows or improve existing systems.

From the analysis, it was found that the overall no show rate for the year 2016 was 20.19%. It would have been beneficial to see the No Show rates for the past 5 years to compare and forecast the future increase. However, since there was only a year worth of data, this was not achievable.
Other findings were as below:

  • The relationship between no shows and gender showed to be inconclusive due to the similarity in no show rate by both sexes. Both male and female clients no showed at the same rate i.e. 20% out of their total appointments, which rules out sex as a variable for no show rate.

  • Before I approached the analysis on Age factor, I was under the assumption that the age group over 57 may have a significant contribution to no showing for their appointments. However after analyzing the data, it was found that the age group over 57 had the least proportion of no shows (i.e. 18%). Whilst, there was a trend found that the younger patients between the age of 1-19 no showed the highest i.e. 30%. The 2nd highest no show was recorded by patients between the ages of 20-38 i.e. 28%.

  • The day of the week also has an effect on the No Show rate. People no showed more towards the beginning of the week more than towards the end of the week. Out of total no shows, the highest proportions of no shows were recorded on Tuesday at 23.1% and the least was recorded on Saturday at 0.04%. Even though Saturday has the least No Shows, there were only 39 appointments booked for the whole year 2106. Out of which 23.7% (9) were missed – which is the highest in comparison to other days.

  • Another interesting fact that was discovered during the analysis was that the SMS reminder did not help in reducing the No Shows. In fact, 28% of missed their appointments despite receiving the SMS which is higher than the one who did not receive i.e. 16.7%. It was also surprising that 83% showed up for their appointments despite no SMS reminder.

After analyzing the factors above, we can conclude that with the given data it will be very difficult to make any moderate/strong prediction of the No Shows based on only one factor.
